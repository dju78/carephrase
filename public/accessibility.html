Accessibility

Accessibility Statement

Published by Daramola Digital Labs  |  Last reviewed: 8 June 2026

Our commitment CarePhrase aims to be usable by everyone, including care workers who use assistive technology. We are working towards WCAG 2.1 AA.

What we have done

  • Semantic HTML headings and landmark regions so screen readers can navigate the content.
  • Labelled form fields and buttons with accessible names.
  • Keyboard-operable controls — you can use CarePhrase without a mouse.
  • A responsive, mobile-first layout that reflows on small screens and when text is enlarged.
  • A light and dark theme that respects your device setting.
  • Clear, plain-English wording suited to a training tool.

Known limitations

CarePhrase is an evolving product and we have not yet completed a full WCAG 2.1 AA audit. Some areas may not yet fully meet the standard. We welcome reports so we can prioritise fixes.

How to use assistive technology

CarePhrase works with standard screen readers, browser zoom, and operating-system text-size and contrast settings. If you rely on these and something does not work as expected, please let us know.

Installing CarePhrase on your phone

CarePhrase can be added to your home screen and used like an app. On iPhone, use the Share button then "Add to Home Screen". On Android, use your browser's "Install app" option.

Reporting an accessibility barrier

If you find an accessibility barrier in CarePhrase, please contact us and we will do our best to put it right. Tell us the page, what you were trying to do, and the assistive technology you were using if relevant.
